Why we want Narcotic free pain management

The most common way of managing patients pain during and after a surgical procedure is the use of Narcotic analgesics. Narcotics attach to receptors on nerves in the brain that increase the general threshold to pain and reduce the perception of pain. They bring a large bevy of unwanted side-effects as strong potential for addiction, low blood pressure, tolerance to pain medication, cardiovascular Effects, Respiratory Problems, even respiratory depression that can lead to death. Specially old and weakened persons suffer from side-effects caused by narcotics. Nevertheless, effective operative and postoperative pain control is an essential component of the care of the surgical patient.

Our Mission

Our mission is to improve pain management in the future through the use of indwelling catheters that block or mitigate pain at the source. Pain management catheters provide excellent postoperative pain relief, especially for shoulder, leg, arm, ankle and knee surgery. They reduce post-operative risks, lead to patients comfort and therefore satisfaction and earlier mobilization. A critical step in effectively inserting a patient's pain management catheter is accurately identifying nerve structures in ultrasound images. Our goal was to have the computer create a region of interest to aid in the detection of this nerve and possibly create a computer guided needle to minimize the pain endured whilst this procedure is performed.
